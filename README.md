## Project Overview
This project simulates a two-month month-end close for a U.S.-based manufacturing company that also provides professional installation and maintenance services. The objective is to mirror real-world corporate accounting and audit workflows under U.S. GAAP, including transaction processing, adjusting entries, reconciliations, and financial reporting.

The project was built alongside CPA exam preparation to strengthen practical application of accrual accounting, revenue recognition, inventory accounting, and internal controls.

---

## Business Scenario
**Pioneer Industrial Tools, Inc.** is a mid-sized company that:
- Manufactures industrial components
- Provides installation and ongoing maintenance services
- Operates using accrual accounting under U.S. GAAP

The company closes its books monthly and prepares financial statements for internal management review and audit readiness.

---

## Scope of Work
This project covers **two accounting periods (February and March 2025)** and includes:
- Raw transaction processing
- Journal entry preparation
- Month-end adjusting entries
- Trial balance generation
- Financial statement preparation
- Bank, AR, and AP reconciliations
- Close checklist and internal controls documentation
- Month-over-month comparative financial analysis

---

## Accounting Areas Covered
- Inventory accounting (Raw Materials, WIP, Finished Goods)
- Cost of Goods Sold (materials, labor, overhead)
- Revenue recognition (product sales and service contracts)
- Deferred revenue and unbilled revenue
- Accruals and deferrals
- Depreciation of manufacturing equipment
- Bad debt estimation
- Interest accruals
- Financial statement presentation
- Internal controls and audit trail documentation

---

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Structured journal entry logs
- Reconciliation schedules
- Accounting documentation and close checklists

The focus of this project is accounting judgment, accuracy, and audit-ready documentation rather than automation.

## Internal Controls & Audit Readiness
This project emphasizes:
- Clear audit trails from source transactions to financial statements
- Consistent chart of accounts across periods
- Reconciliation and review procedures
- Documented close processes

These practices reflect those commonly observed in corporate accounting and public audit environments.
